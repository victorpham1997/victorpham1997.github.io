Rehabilitation Game for Physically Impared Patients

A fun experience to help patients rehabilitate

About the Project:
The aim of the project was to develop an interactive game for physcially impared patients to rehabilitate during and after treatment. In this project, we focus on upper body limbs and hand-eye coordination training.

We developed 3 games using motion sensing devices such as Myo Armband and Leap Motion and eye tracking decive: Tobii Eyex. The games run on Unity3D engine.

This project was done under A*STAR Research Attachment Programme (ARAP) in a group of 2 students.


The first game required user to use his arm to move the cursor to the highlighted object while looking at it and squeeze his hand to score the point.
This is to help the training of hand-eye coordination and hand muscle.

The second game required user to collect the ball from one of the three colour box and drop it into the respective colour box.
This is to help the training of hand rotation and reflex.

The third game required user to carry out a series of hand movement as required by the number indicated. User has to either squeeze the hand or relax it as instructed by the game.
This is to help the training of arm stretching and hand muscle.
